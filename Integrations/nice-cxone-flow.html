Flowchart illustrating how calls are routed from NICE CXone to a Retell AI voice agent via SIP trunk,
with call context passed in both directions and outcomes logged back to NICE for unified reporting.
How it works
1
Inbound call arrives. A customer calls into the contact center. The call lands on the NICE CXone platform as it does today.
2
NICE runs IVR and queue routing. The call passes through NICE's existing IVR flow and skills based routing logic. Based on the queue or skill assignment, NICE determines this call should be handled by a Retell AI agent.
3
Call routes to Retell via SIP trunk. NICE sends the call audio to Retell through an auto provisioned SIP trunk, along with call context and metadata such as caller ID, account number, queue name, and IVR selections.
4
AI agent handles the call. The Retell AI voice agent conducts the conversation, uses connected integrations (CRM lookup, ticket creation, booking), and resolves the customer's request.
5
Transfer to human if needed. If the AI agent cannot resolve the issue, it transfers the call back to a human agent in NICE with full conversation context, summary, and collected data so the human can pick up seamlessly.
6
Human agent continues. The human agent in NICE takes over the call with full context. From the customer's perspective, it is one continuous conversation.
7
Call outcome logged back to NICE. After the call ends, Retell logs the outcome, disposition, and analytics back to NICE so the enterprise has unified reporting across both human and AI handled calls.
